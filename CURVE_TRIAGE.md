# RBC Curve Triage Guide

## Title and purpose

This document is a working technical note for curve triage in the RBC metabolic calibration project.

Its purpose is not only to reduce aggregate loss, but to classify the type of mismatch behind each metabolite curve before opening more parameters. In this project, a lower score is useful only if it corresponds to a better physiological explanation of the observed storage-lesion trajectories.

The central triage question is:

- is a mismatch mainly a local parameter-fitting problem,
- a transport or observation-model problem,
- or a deeper structural problem in the reduced RBC network?

This distinction matters because the current calibration system can improve fit by opening parameters that compensate numerically without improving biological credibility. Curve triage is therefore the front end of staged calibration, not a reporting afterthought.

## Triage categories

### Physiological anchors

These are high-value metabolites whose trajectories summarize whether the model is reproducing the main storage-lesion physiology. They should stabilize first and should remain protected while later families are opened.

Typical examples are extracellular and intracellular glucose/lactate, adenylates, 2,3-BPG, and glutathione redox state.

### Likely parametric

These metabolites are usually controlled by a relatively compact set of reaction capacities or Michaelis constants already exposed in calibration. A mismatch here often means the corresponding pathway kinetics are still poorly tuned rather than fundamentally missing.

These are appropriate early-stage optimization targets.

### Likely transport / observation-driven

These metabolites can mismatch because the dominant signal is not the internal metabolic state alone. Export, import, bag accumulation, irreversible leakage, extracellular instability, or incomplete observation mapping may dominate the curve.

A good fit here should usually be interpreted only after the corresponding intracellular precursor family is behaving well.

### Likely structural

These metabolites should be treated cautiously when their mismatch appears to reflect missing pathways, over-lumped side chemistry, disabled reactions in the RBC-strict setup, or weak experimental observability. Large improvements in these curves can be artificial if they are achieved before the energy and glycolysis anchors are stable.

## Per-metabolite triage entries

### `EGLC`

- **Family:** core benchmark, transport anchor
- **Biological role:** extracellular glucose supply available to the cell during storage
- **Controlling reactions:** `VEGLC` with opposing intracellular demand from `VHK`
- **Expected curve behavior:** gradual depletion rather than abrupt collapse; shape should remain consistent with intracellular glucose handling
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven first, parametric second
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** tune `vmax_VEGLC`, `km_EGLC`, and `km_GLC_transport` only together with `VHK`; do not interpret `EGLC` in isolation from `GLC`

### `GLC`

- **Family:** core benchmark, glycolysis entry
- **Biological role:** intracellular glucose pool feeding glycolysis and PPP entry
- **Controlling reactions:** `VEGLC`, `VHK`, indirectly `VPGI` and downstream pull through `VPFK`
- **Expected curve behavior:** buffered intracellular pool, not persistent overshoot if import and phosphorylation are balanced
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric balance between transport and hexokinase; transport mismatch if extracellular and intracellular curves separate unrealistically
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** fit glucose import and hexokinase together before opening broad side pathways; reject solutions where `GLC` improves only because transport is throttled while glycolysis remains weak

### `ELAC`

- **Family:** core benchmark, extracellular export anchor
- **Biological role:** extracellular accumulation of lactate generated by glycolysis
- **Controlling reactions:** `VELAC`, upstream `VLDH`, `VPK`, and overall glycolytic throughput
- **Expected curve behavior:** rising exported product, with shape reflecting sustained glycolytic production rather than isolated transport tuning
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven if `ELAC` mismatches while `LAC` is reasonable; otherwise upstream glycolytic parametric mismatch
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** use `ELAC` as a consistency check on `LAC` and `PYR`; avoid fitting `VELAC` aggressively before `VPK` and `VLDH` are credible

### `LAC`

- **Family:** core benchmark, glycolytic end product
- **Biological role:** intracellular lactate produced from pyruvate through `VLDH`
- **Controlling reactions:** `VLDH`, `VPK`, `VELAC`, and the upstream glycolytic chain
- **Expected curve behavior:** accumulation coupled to pyruvate generation and export; should not decouple from `ELAC`
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric if pyruvate-to-lactate conversion is wrong, transport-driven if export dominates
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** fit `VPK`, `VLDH`, `km_PYR`, and `km_LAC` before opening compensatory sinks such as `VPEP_PASE`

### `ATP`

- **Family:** core benchmark, energy anchor
- **Biological role:** main energy currency and the strongest sanity check on global physiology
- **Controlling reactions:** production by `VPGK` and `VPK`; consumption by `VHK`, `VPFK`, `VAK2`, `VGMPS`, `VGLNS`, `VGSS`, `VOPLAH`, and other ATP-using reactions; buffering by `VAK` and `VAK_rev`
- **Expected curve behavior:** decline over storage without catastrophic early collapse; endpoint and curve shape both matter
- **Likely mismatch type:** structural only after major ATP producers and consumers are already well tuned; otherwise parametric
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** treat ATP protection as a hard gate; reject any improvement elsewhere that breaks ATP trajectory or final pool retention

### `ADP`

- **Family:** core benchmark, adenylate anchor
- **Biological role:** immediate coupling state between ATP production and ATP consumption
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPGK`, `VPK`, `VHK`, `VPFK`, `VAK`, `VAK_rev`, `VAK2`, and the shared ATP/ADP handling terms
- **Expected curve behavior:** coordinated with ATP and AMP, not free to drift independently
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric in adenylate interconversion and glycolytic energy turnover
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** evaluate only with the full adenylate trio; do not keep a run with improved ADP if ATP collapses or AMP becomes nonphysical

### `AMP`

- **Family:** core benchmark, adenylate and purine bridge
- **Biological role:** low-energy adenylate state and entry point into purine loss through deamination
- **Controlling reactions:** `VAK`, `VAK_rev`, `VAK2`, `VAMPD1`, `VAPRT`, `VADSL`, and ATP-consuming branches that drain the pool
- **Expected curve behavior:** coordinated with ATP and ADP; should reflect pool depletion without implausible AMP trapping
- **Likely mismatch type:** mixed parametric and pathway-coupling mismatch
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** fit AMP only in an ATP-protected setting; do not open broader purine exits until adenylate shape is acceptable

### `B23PG`

- **Family:** core benchmark, RBC-specific anchor
- **Biological role:** 2,3-bisphosphoglycerate shunt metabolite and one of the most RBC-specific curve checks
- **Controlling reactions:** `VDPGM`, `V23DPGP`, upstream `VPGK`, and glycolytic supply through `B13PG`
- **Expected curve behavior:** controlled decline or redistribution, not explosive accumulation
- **Likely mismatch type:** strongly parametric in shunt balance
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** calibrate `vmax_VDPGM`, `vmax_V23DPGP`, and related glycolytic pull before introducing late compensators; keep this metabolite as an explicit acceptance anchor

### `GSH`

- **Family:** core benchmark, redox anchor
- **Biological role:** reduced glutathione pool representing antioxidant capacity
- **Controlling reactions:** synthesis through `VGLUCYS` and `VGSS`; recycling through `VGSR`; consumption and turnover through `VGPX`, `VGGT`, and `VGGCT`
- **Expected curve behavior:** gradual depletion under storage stress, not arbitrary oscillation
- **Likely mismatch type:** mixed parametric and structural, because it depends on both redox and side-metabolism closure
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** tune `VGSR`, `VGSS`, `VGPX`, `VGGT`, and `km_GSSG` conservatively; do not let GSH fit be achieved through unrelated side-pathway leakage

### `GSSG`

- **Family:** core benchmark, redox anchor
- **Biological role:** oxidized glutathione reflecting oxidative burden and recycling capacity
- **Controlling reactions:** `VGPX`, `VGSR`, with possible interaction from `VEGSSG` only for extracellular export readouts
- **Expected curve behavior:** should move consistently with GSH and not imply impossible redox recovery
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric if recycling is weak, structural if redox coupling remains wrong even when ATP and glutathione synthesis are reasonable
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 1
- **Recommended next actions:** fit internal redox first, then interpret any extracellular glutathione-related readouts separately

### `G6P`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** first committed intracellular glucose product and branch point into PPP
- **Controlling reactions:** `VHK`, `VPGI`, `VG6PDH`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should not accumulate uncontrollably if entry and downstream pull are balanced
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** use `G6P` to diagnose whether mismatch sits at glycolysis entry or PPP diversion; do not jump to side metabolism

### `F6P`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** intermediate between `G6P` and `F16BP`
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPGI`, `VPFK`, `VTKL2`, `VTAL`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should track a balanced throughput state, not accumulate far above `F16BP`
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric, especially `VPFK`
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** use as a direct check on whether `VPFK` is keeping pace with upstream supply

### `F16BP`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** committed upper-glycolysis intermediate and feedforward regulator of PK in the model
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPFK`, `VFDPA`, and indirect PK activation through `ka_F16BP_PK`, `alpha_F16BP_PK`, `n_F16BP_PK`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should rise and fall with upper glycolysis, not remain trapped
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric, but can become compensatory if PK regulation is overused
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** fit `VPFK` and `VFDPA` first; treat PK allosteric terms as secondary refinements, not primary rescue knobs

### `P2G`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** lower-glycolysis intermediate upstream of PEP
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPGM`, `VENOPGM`, indirect feedback from PEP accumulation through enolase inhibition logic
- **Expected curve behavior:** should not accumulate persistently if lower glycolysis is open
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** use `P2G` as a direct diagnostic for enolase bottleneck before opening bypass sinks

### `PEP`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** immediate substrate for pyruvate kinase and a sensitive lower-glycolysis bottleneck indicator
- **Controlling reactions:** `VENOPGM`, `VPK`, and the bypass `VPEP_PASE`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should not be rescued mainly by nonphysiological shunting
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric early, compensator-sensitive later
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** keep `VPEP_PASE` restricted early; first solve `VENOPGM` and `VPK`

### `PYR`

- **Family:** internal glycolysis
- **Biological role:** endpoint of glycolysis before lactate conversion or export
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPK`, `VLDH`, `VEPYR`, `VALATA_rev`, `VME`, and `VPEP_PASE` indirectly
- **Expected curve behavior:** should remain coupled to lactate production; persistent pyruvate excess usually means lower-glycolysis imbalance
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric first, structural only later
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 2
- **Recommended next actions:** use together with `LAC` and `ELAC` to decide whether the issue is PK, LDH, or export balance

### `IMP`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** central branch point between adenylate degradation and salvage/redirection
- **Controlling reactions:** `VAMPD1`, `VHGPRT1`, `VADSS`, `VIMPH`, `Vnucleo2`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should reflect controlled adenylate loss, not unrestricted accumulation
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric once ATP and AMP are stable
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** do not optimize IMP aggressively before adenylate core is acceptable; then use IMP to separate AMP deamination from salvage imbalance

### `INO`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** nucleoside intermediate linking IMP and hypoxanthine with possible export
- **Controlling reactions:** `VADA`, `Vnucleo2`, `VPNPase1`, `VEINO`
- **Expected curve behavior:** modest intermediate behavior rather than uncontrolled sink or source behavior
- **Likely mismatch type:** mixed parametric and transport-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** first stabilize IMP and HYPX internally; only then tune `VEINO`

### `HYPX`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** hypoxanthine is a key degradation product and salvage substrate
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPNPase1`, `VHGPRT1`, `VXAO`, `VEHYPX`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should rise with purine degradation but remain bounded by salvage and oxidation
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric with transport sensitivity
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** fit internal production and salvage before export; use HYPX as a bridge metabolite, not an isolated target

### `XAN`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** downstream oxidation product with very small-scale experimental values and strong sensitivity to outlet balance
- **Controlling reactions:** `VXAO`, `VXAO2`, `VEXAN`, `VGDA`
- **Expected curve behavior:** small and tightly constrained; easy to overfit numerically
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven and structurally fragile
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** keep `VEXAN` and `VGDA` heavily constrained until upstream purine logic is stable; do not use XAN as an early success criterion

### `URT`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** urate end-product and export-facing purine readout
- **Controlling reactions:** `VXAO2`, `VEURT`
- **Expected curve behavior:** downstream accumulation consistent with xanthine oxidation and efflux
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** interpret only after `XAN` and `HYPX` are credible; do not open `VEURT` early as a free score improver

### `GMP`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** guanylate pool member reflecting salvage and GMP branch balance
- **Controlling reactions:** `VGMPS`, `VHGPRT2`, `VGMPK`, `Vnucleo_GMP`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should not be emptied only to improve downstream xanthine metrics
- **Likely mismatch type:** parametric with high compensator risk
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** only open GMP degradation and side exits late; use GMP mainly to check whether guanylate balance remains plausible

### `ADE`

- **Family:** purines
- **Biological role:** adenine salvage substrate and possible leakage/export-sensitive pool
- **Controlling reactions:** `VAPRT`, `VEADE`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should reflect salvage pressure and external exchange, not a free-standing internal pathway signal
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 3
- **Recommended next actions:** interpret ADE together with AMP behavior and extracellular adenine; do not force ADE fit by over-opening `VEADE`

### `GLU`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** central amino-acid and glutathione-cycle intermediate
- **Controlling reactions:** `VGDH`, `VGDH_rev`, `VGLNS`, `VASPTA`, `VALATA`, `VOPLAH`, `VEGLU`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should reflect coupled nitrogen and redox handling, not independent free movement
- **Likely mismatch type:** likely structural or over-lumped side-network mismatch
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** keep GLU closed until ATP, glutathione, and purines are stable; interpret large GLU corrections skeptically

### `GLN`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** glutamine pool linking ammonia handling and GMP synthesis
- **Controlling reactions:** `VGLNS`, `VGMPS`, `VEGLN`
- **Expected curve behavior:** moderate coupling to nitrogen handling rather than independent benchmark behavior
- **Likely mismatch type:** transport / observation-driven with structural sensitivity
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** only open after purine and redox cores are behaving; do not tune `VEGLN` before internal GLU/GLN balance is credible

### `SER`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** one-carbon and amino-acid metabolite connected to 3-phosphoglycerate
- **Controlling reactions:** `VPHGDH`, `VSHMT`, `VCBS`, `VESER`
- **Expected curve behavior:** should be treated as a late side-family readout, not a primary glycolysis benchmark
- **Likely mismatch type:** structural or compensator-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** open only after lower glycolysis is stable; avoid using `VPHGDH` as an early sink for `P3G`-family mismatch

### `SAH`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** one-carbon / methylation-cycle intermediate
- **Controlling reactions:** `VSAM`, `VSAH`, `VAHCY`
- **Expected curve behavior:** slow side-cycle behavior, not a dominant calibration anchor
- **Likely mismatch type:** likely structural
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** interpret qualitatively unless core anchors are already stable; do not accept a run because SAH improved while ATP or glucose/lactate worsened

### `OXOP`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** gamma-glutamyl-cycle intermediate linked to glutathione turnover
- **Controlling reactions:** `VGGCT`, `VOPLAH`, `VEOXOP`
- **Expected curve behavior:** side-cycle accumulation sensitive to both intracellular turnover and export
- **Likely mismatch type:** structural and transport / observation-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** open late and under regularization; treat `VOPLAH` as a restricted clean-up reaction rather than an early optimizer degree of freedom

### `MAL`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** malate pool connected to malic enzyme, malate dehydrogenase, fumarate, and export
- **Controlling reactions:** `VME`, `VMLD`, `VFUM`, `VEMAL`
- **Expected curve behavior:** coupled to side redox and anaplerotic surrogates; not a primary anchor in the mature RBC-strict setting
- **Likely mismatch type:** structural or transport / observation-driven
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** delay MAL-focused fitting until glycolysis, ATP, and purines are already acceptable

### `CIT`

- **Family:** side metabolism
- **Biological role:** citrate readout with limited active internal turnover in the current RBC-strict model
- **Controlling reactions:** `VECIT`; TCA-like internal conversions such as `VACO` and `VACLY` are disabled in the active RBC-strict setup
- **Expected curve behavior:** should be interpreted cautiously because transport dominates in the current structural configuration
- **Likely mismatch type:** likely structural
- **Calibration priority:** Priority 4
- **Recommended next actions:** do not let citrate mismatch drive early calibration decisions; treat it as a late structural diagnostic

## Dangerous compensator parameters

Some parameters can lower loss without improving the physiological credibility of the run. These should be opened late, kept on tight bounds, or regularized more strongly.

- `vmax_VPEP_PASE`
  - This creates an ADP-independent escape route from `PEP` to `PYR`.
  - It can hide a real `VENOPGM` or `VPK` bottleneck while preserving lower-glycolysis loss artificially.

- secondary `VE*` transport parameters
  - Export terms such as `VEXAN`, `VEURT`, `VEINO`, `VEADE`, `VEGLN`, `VEGLU`, `VEOXOP`, `VESER`, `VEMAL`, and similar transporters can improve extracellular curves without fixing the internal pathway state that produced them.
  - These are useful only after the corresponding internal family is already plausible.

- `vmax_Vnucleo_GMP`
  - This opens a guanylate pool exit and can improve purine loss numerically by draining `GMP`.
  - It should not be used early to solve guanylate mismatch.

- `vmax_VGDA`
  - This pushes guanine toward xanthine and can improve downstream purine readouts while worsening pool realism.
  - It is a late-stage control, not an early benchmark lever.

- `vmax_VPHGDH`
  - This can act as a side sink from the phosphoglycerate region into serine metabolism.
  - It should not be opened before core glycolysis is stable.

- `vmax_VOPLAH`
  - This can clear `OXOP` and perturb ATP usage in the side glutathione cycle.
  - It should be opened only after glutathione and ATP anchors are already acceptable.

The general rule is:

- first solve the anchor family with direct core kinetics,
- then allow late side exits only if the anchor improvement survives physiological checks.

## Calibration roadmap from triage

### Stage 1: core benchmark

- **Target metabolites:** `EGLC`, `GLC`, `ELAC`, `LAC`, `ATP`, `ADP`, `AMP`, `B23PG`, `GSH`, `GSSG`
- **Recommended strategy type:** `vmax_then_km`
- **What should be learned before moving on:**
  - whether glucose import and intracellular glucose use are balanced,
  - whether lactate production/export is consistent,
  - whether ATP is protected,
  - whether the 2,3-BPG shunt and glutathione redox state behave plausibly

### Stage 2: glycolysis benchmark

- **Target metabolites:** Stage 1 targets plus `G6P`, `F6P`, `F16BP`, `P2G`, `PEP`, `PYR`
- **Recommended strategy type:** `vmax_then_km` or a tightly bounded `joint_vmax_km` only after Stage 1 is stable
- **What should be learned before moving on:**
  - where upper- versus lower-glycolysis bottlenecks remain,
  - whether PK regulation is being used appropriately,
  - whether lower-glycolysis mismatch is being solved through real pathway balance rather than `VPEP_PASE`

### Stage 3: purine benchmark

- **Target metabolites:** Stage 2 targets plus `IMP`, `INO`, `HYPX`, `XAN`, `URT`, `GMP`, `ADE`
- **Recommended strategy type:** staged purine opening after the energy core is protected; avoid broad late-side transport at first
- **What should be learned before moving on:**
  - whether adenylate depletion and salvage are balanced,
  - whether purine export is a consequence of internal state rather than a free fit parameter,
  - whether guanylate-side exits are being used as compensators

### Stage 4: full benchmark with side metabolism and possible observation model

- **Target metabolites:** all previous targets plus `GLU`, `GLN`, `SER`, `SAH`, `OXOP`, `MAL`, `CIT`, and secondary extracellular readouts
- **Recommended strategy type:** `staged_full`, but with side parameters and transport families opened only after the previous stages pass acceptance checks
- **What should be learned before moving on:**
  - which remaining mismatches are truly kinetic,
  - which are dominated by transport or extracellular observation effects,
  - which reflect structural limits of the current RBC-strict network and therefore should not drive core recalibration

## Keep / discard criteria

### Keep

- improved anchor curves in the Priority 1 family
- no ATP collapse
- better endpoint behavior for protected metabolites
- better curve-shape agreement, not just better average level matching
- purine improvements that preserve the energy core
- extracellular improvements that are consistent with the corresponding intracellular precursor

### Discard

- global score improves only through compensatory side pathways
- purine fit improves while the energy core degrades
- level fit improves but time-course shape does not
- extracellular curves improve only because transport is over-opened while internal state remains implausible
- side-metabolism fits improve at the cost of worsening glucose/lactate, adenylates, `B23PG`, or glutathione anchors

- Core adenylate behavior remains unstable

- Improvement is mostly numerical and not physiologically convincing

- remove `km_B13PG` from the anchor stage
- freeze or remove `km_GLC_HK` from the anchor stage
- rerun the focused core benchmark
